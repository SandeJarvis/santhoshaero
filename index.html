About Me

Hello, I’m Santhosh.

Aerospace engineer focused on aerodynamics, CFD, experimental flow visualization, and conceptual design of aircraft & UAVs.

I’m driven by curiosity about how air interacts with shape, motion, and performance. My work spans aerodynamics, CFD, lab methods, and design. I currently work at the Leibniz Institute for Plasma Science and Technology (INP Greifswald), exploring cold plasma–flow interactions and developing automated experimental systems that improve precision and repeatability in research.

Previously at The ePlane Company, I combined simulation and experiments to advance eVTOL aerodynamics—building rigs for thrust/torque measurement, validating CFD, and iterating on ducts and stators. Earlier roles at IIT Kanpur and Laboratoire PRISME (France) sharpened my understanding of unsteady aerodynamics, flow visualization, and experimental fluid mechanics.

I enjoy bridging simulation and experimentation using coding, ROS, IoT, and rapid prototyping to make complex systems smarter and more reliable. This site is my evolving engineering diary—where I collect ideas, tools, and design perspectives as time allows.
